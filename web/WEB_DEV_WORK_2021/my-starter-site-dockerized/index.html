My Summer So Far

My summer has been pretty good so far. I have a decent job and have had plenty of time to do the activities I enjoy, like lifting and coding.

With all my free time, I've started taking lifting more seriously and have taken measures to optimize a lean bulk up. I've increased my protein and overall food consumption, while cutting out most junk food and sticking to a balanced diet (I've even been eating more vegetables recently). For workouts, I've been doing a pull-push-legs schedule with a rest day after leg day. This is slightly more rest days than I've been doing previously, which can get boring sometimes, but hopefully they will help my gains in the long run by giving my muscles more time to recover. Speaking of muscle recovery, I've also been trying to get more sleep; at least 7 hours a night is my ideal target (I don't always reach this when I have to wake up early for work, however). I've also been napping more, which has felt great.

For coding, I've made it my goal to master HTML, CSS, and Javascript before I go back to school. This might be a bit overambitious simply because I procrastinated until July to start learning, and I've also wasted considerable time writing these paragraphs.

Certifications to look at

Java Foundations certified Junior Associate

Earning the Java Foundations Certified Junior Associate arm you with evidence of your knowledge of the fundamentals of Java programming. Oracle Junior Associate Certifications can be a helpful credential when applying for internships, summer jobs, and your first job leaving post-secondary education as well as preparing you to progress into Oracle Certified Associate level, and later to Oracle Certified Professional. Oracle Certified Junior-Associate certification mainly focused on young people who want to study computer science in the future. It is a perfect first step to get the stone rolling. If you are seeking a more career-level certification, you may consider Java SE 8 Programmer I | 1Z0-808 or Java SE 7 Programmer I |1Z0-803.
